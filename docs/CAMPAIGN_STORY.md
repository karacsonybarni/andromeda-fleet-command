# The Black Sun Incident

The playable vertical slice is one connected three-chapter story. Admiral Sera
Vey guides the player from a rescue patrol to the discovery of a Ketzal invasion
corridor. Each victory answers the immediate question and creates the next
mission's objective; defeat text explains what was lost without changing the
canonical campaign path.

## Chapter progression

| Chapter | Story turn | Player fleet | Enemies | Simultaneous threat groups | Command lesson |
| --- | --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | --- |

## Story beats

### Chapter I — First Command

Nysa Seven discovers an encoded star map in a raider beacon. The player must
save the trapped convoy, destroy the raider leader, and recover its command
core. Victory identifies a Ketzal military cipher, the phrase “Black Sun,” and
a destination: Pelagos Corridor.

### Chapter II — Broken Shield

Carrier One is decoding the stolen cipher when multiple bomber wings attack
from separate axes. The player must expand from a two-ship detachment to a
four-role fleet, protect the carrier, and destroy every bomber. Victory reveals
that Black Sun is not a ship name but a signal masking an active invasion
corridor.

### Chapter III — Black Sun

The player intercepts the layered Ketzal fleet sustaining the corridor. The
flagship, carrier, destroyers, bombers, and escorts create overlapping threats,
requiring full-fleet coordination. Destroying the Ketzal flagship collapses the
corridor and completes the incident. Its final transmission—“Crown Fleet,
awaken”—is a sequel hook, not a promise that more campaign missions are already
implemented.

## Scope boundary

This is the complete narrative arc for the current three-mission playable
vertical slice. A longer Steam campaign, professional voice performances, and
mission-duration tuning remain future work and should not be represented as
features available in the current build.
