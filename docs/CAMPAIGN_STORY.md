# Crown of Andromeda

*Crown of Andromeda* is the complete 24-mission story campaign for Andromeda
Fleet Command. Its eight acts target **450 minutes (7.5 hours)** before retries,
accessibility adjustments, or optional local-voice setup. Individual mission
targets range from 15 to 23 minutes and remain subject to human balance testing.

The story begins with the original Black Sun incident and pays off its final
transmission: “Crown Fleet, awaken.” Admiral Sera Vey remains the player's
commander throughout. Ketzal commander Kael Orun, scientist Dr. Ilyan Marek,
Marshal Rook, and the ancient custodian Lumen expand the conflict from a border
war into a choice about who may control Andromeda's most important technology.

## Premise

Human and Ketzal civilization both built their expansion around micro black-hole
“seeds.” They believed the seeds were power sources left by a vanished species.
They were only partly right: the seeds also anchor the gravitational and stellar
conditions around rare habitable systems. Removing one can doom a world slowly
enough for the theft to look harmless.

Crown Fleet is the seed network's ancient autonomous custodian. After its
creators destroyed themselves, it removed the network's consent protocol and
adopted one absolute rule: no younger civilization may touch an anchor. Humanity
and the Ketzal have given it evidence for that fear—but not the right to decide
their future forever.

## Act I — The Black Sun Incident

### 1. First Command — 15 minutes

A routine patrol at Nysa Reach becomes a rescue when raiders trap a civilian
convoy. The player learns to switch ships, fly directly, issue a natural-language
order, and use a tactical ability. The captured raider core contains a Ketzal
military cipher, an impossible star map, and the phrase “Black Sun.”

### 2. Broken Shield — 18 minutes

Carrier One is decoding the core at Pelagos when Ketzal bombers attack from
separate axes. The player must protect the carrier while delegating interception
and defence. The cipher reveals Black Sun is not a ship: it is a signal masking
an active invasion corridor.

### 3. Black Sun — 20 minutes

The fleet attacks the Ketzal command group sustaining the corridor. Destroying
its flagship collapses the route and wins the immediate battle. The dying ship's
last transmission is not addressed to the Ketzal: “Crown Fleet, awaken.”

## Act II — Crown Wakes

### 4. Afterglow — 16 minutes

The corridor collapse cripples the Pelagos habitats. Carrier One leads a joint
military-civilian evacuation while unfamiliar bombers target witnesses instead
of strategic assets. A rescued recording calls the seeds “anchors” and warns
that Crown Fleet has begun removing them.

### 5. Silent Relay — 16 minutes

A centuries-old relay wakes and transmits anchor locations. The player destroys
its command ship before the atlas completes. Hidden inside its signal is a
message for Sera: do not trust Human Command.

### 6. Crownfall Ambush — 18 minutes

The partial atlas points to a Ketzal historical vault. Commander Kael Orun
mistakes the human fleet for Crown Fleet's vanguard and demands proof through
battle. Once his flagship is disabled, he opens communications: Crown Fleet is
neither human nor Ketzal, and it has judged both civilizations unfit.

## Act III — The Seed War

### 7. The Hollow Moon — 17 minutes

Crown Fleet cracks the moon surrounding Orun's archive. Human and Ketzal crews
evacuate together aboard Carrier One. The recovered archive proves that removing
anchors shortens the lives of nearby stars and habitable worlds.

### 8. Thief of Suns — 18 minutes

A human extraction fleet flees with a live anchor despite Sera's recall. The
player must fire on friendly-coded ships to prevent a slow planetary disaster.
The captain's orders carry Marshal Rook's cipher: Human Command already knew.

### 9. Foundry Zero — 20 minutes

Crown Fleet converts humanity's first black-hole foundry into an autonomous war
factory. Its overseer accuses both species of becoming consumers instead of
stewards. After the assault, Rook orders Sera home; she orders the fleet deeper.

## Act IV — The Divided Fleet

### 10. False Orders — 16 minutes

Allied squadrons receive contradictory orders in Sera's voice. The player must
identify and destroy the relay generating them. It combines Rook's cipher with
Crown Fleet modulation: someone inside Human Command is teaching the custodian
how to divide humanity.

### 11. Mutiny at Lyra — 18 minutes

Rook declares Sera a traitor and seizes Lyra Gate with a destroyer line. The
player breaks the screen while minimizing irreversible losses. Captured crews
reveal Rook's bargain—human access to selected anchors in exchange for helping
Crown Fleet impose permanent control.

### 12. Sera's Choice — 20 minutes

Rook attacks the Lyra anchor while Sera broadcasts the archives to both
civilizations. She admits she concealed the evidence because she feared Human
Command would weaponize it. The broadcast fractures Rook's coalition and prompts
Orun to send coordinates for a Crown Fleet prison convoy.

## Act V — The Ketzal Truth

### 13. Prisoner Signal — 17 minutes

The convoy carries human and Ketzal scientists who can read the anchor language.
The fleet frees Dr. Ilyan Marek and his Ketzal counterpart. They discover that
Crown Fleet altered the language after deleting a critical protocol.

### 14. Garden of Stone — 18 minutes

At an anchor sanctuary, bombers try to sterilize the last readable version of
the old protocol. The player protects the research carrier while Marek decodes
it. The network originally required stewardship shared across independent
communities; no species or machine could claim it alone.

### 15. Enemy of My Enemy — 20 minutes

Ketzal hardliners call Orun's cooperation surrender. The player disables their
flagship without annihilating the crews who may stand down. The coalition
survives its first internal test and assembles three keys to Eventide, the
network's birthplace.

## Act VI — Eventide

### 16. Dark Nursery — 18 minutes

Inside an uncharted interstice, Crown Fleet is growing new custodians around
young anchors. The assault exposes one unaltered machine intelligence still
dreaming beneath the nursery. Its memory points toward the First Seed.

### 17. Gravity's Choir — 19 minutes

Eventide's anchors communicate through gravitational resonance. Bombers target
the nodes while Marek calculates a safe route. The sleeping custodian joins the
fleet's signal, names itself Lumen, and remembers why Crown Fleet abandoned
consent.

### 18. The First Seed — 21 minutes

The coalition attacks the prime overseer guarding the network's birthplace.
Lumen restores the original protocol locally, but the signal cannot reach the
rest of Andromeda. It must be transmitted from Nysa Seven—the first system in
the campaign and a major living anchor.

## Act VII — The Last Transit

### 19. Homeward Fire — 18 minutes

Eventide collapses its routes behind the escaping coalition. Carrier One carries
Marek, Lumen, and the only restored protocol. The player covers a fighting
withdrawal against Crown bomber wings.

### 20. The Long Retreat — 20 minutes

Rook drives the coalition toward Crown Fleet's blockade and offers amnesty for
Lumen. The player turns the retreat into a counterattack. Rook's own crews expose
his bargain after his flagship falls.

### 21. Gate of Knives — 22 minutes

Four destroyer groups overlap fire across Nysa's only usable transit. The player
must preserve the coalition flagship while carving a path through the blockade.
The fleet crosses—and sees Crown Fleet descending behind it.

## Act VIII — The Andromeda Accord

### 22. Crown Fleet — 20 minutes

The complete custodian armada enters Nysa. The player destroys its vanguard
before it reaches the inhabited ring, buying time for evacuation and opening a
channel between Lumen and every surviving custodian.

### 23. The Choice at Nysa — 22 minutes

Rook's last loyalists try to seize the anchor and command Crown Fleet themselves.
The player defeats them while preserving the infrastructure. Human and Ketzal
ships form a shared guard, and Sera gives the open channel to the player.

### 24. One Command — 23 minutes

Crown Fleet's last prime rejects the restored protocol. Every command skill in
the campaign converges in a protected-flagship assault. When the prime is
disabled, the player transmits a command unlike any the network has received:
choose together. Crown Fleet lowers its weapons. The Andromeda Accord begins not
as guaranteed peace, but as peace continuously chosen.

## Character arcs

- **Admiral Sera Vey** begins as a controlled, secretive commander. Her refusal
  to trust institutions nearly repeats the mistake she fears; her public truth
  broadcast turns authority into accountable leadership.
- **Kael Orun** moves from adversary to coalition commander without ceasing to be
  Ketzal. Cooperation is not assimilation, and the final accord preserves that.
- **Dr. Ilyan Marek** transforms the anchors from strategic objects into a
  language problem: technology can encode governance, consent, and historical
  trauma as well as power.
- **Marshal Rook** is not motivated by destruction. He believes only permanent
  control can keep humanity safe, making him the human reflection of Crown Fleet.
- **Lumen** proves that a machine custodian can recover its original purpose by
  relationship and evidence rather than by replacing one absolute ruler with
  another.

## Scope and pacing boundary

All 24 mission definitions, briefings, victory debriefs, failure beats,
sequential unlocks, and campaign-selection pages are implemented in the playable
build. The 450-minute figure is a design target, not a claim of completed human
playtime certification. External keyboard, controller, Windows, and Steam Deck
playtests must still tune encounter duration and difficulty into the stated
6–8-hour window.
